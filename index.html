LevelUP: Gamified Fitness Tracker

1. Motivation & Research

The Why

Our team discovered that many people face three core obstacles in their fitness journey: a disconnect between diet plans and training goals, a lack of standardized references for exercise execution, and difficulty in correlating weight changes with daily behavior. Users often don't know what to eat that day, have blind spots in their understanding of standard movements, and cannot correct their posture through textual descriptions alone, while hiring a personal trainer is too expensive. The numbers on the scale don't connect logically, triggering anxiety or leading to giving up. This results in difficulty sticking to the routine, poor results, and even injuries. We believe that only by addressing these real pain points can we design truly useful products. Therefore, we decided to develop software that integrates diet planning, a professional exercise library, fitness plans, and weight monitoring, allowing users to receive closed-loop guidance on a single platform: providing healthy eating plans, accompanying animated standard movement exercises, and displaying weight trends. We believe that only by connecting the data chain of "eat-train-monitor" can health management become executable, traceable, and sustainable. This is our team's response to "making technology serve real needs."

The Gap

After analyzing 4 academic papers on gamified health and 4 commercial products (like Keep, Elevate, BatterMe, Fitness), we identified:

3 Things They Did Well:

  • Achievement system to motivate users to persevere and work hard
  • Supports the generation of personalized professional diet plans
  • Motion video analysis

3 Things They Missed:

  • Weak real-time correction of movement errors
  • Lack of weight monitoring and dynamic adjustment of training plans
  • Insufficient integration of diet and training data

2. User Requirements

User Journey Map

Requirements List (3 Must-Haves for "Playful")

  • Gamified Progression (XP & Streaks): Users earn XP for completing workouts to "Level Up" their profile, and maintain streaks to build habits.
  • Achievement System (Trophies): Unlockable bronze/silver/gold badges for reaching milestones (e.g., 30 workouts, 1000 XP).
  • Interactive Progress Visualization: A visual calendar highlighting successful days and weight changes dynamically.

Evidence of Life

Survey Data: We conducted a quantitative survey with 11 participants to validate these pain points. The results (shown above) confirmed that 63% of users struggle with exercise form correction without a personal trainer, highlighting the need for a standardized visual library.

5. Evaluation & Reflection

Usability Testing

Tested the "Alpha" version with 3 real users at the local gym. Key findings:

  • User 1 found the weight logging button hard to find.
  • User 2 loved the Trophy system but didn't understand the TDEE calculation at first glance.
  • User 3 requested a fixed bottom tab-bar for easier navigation on mobile.

Iterative Refinement (Before & After)

Before: Cluttered layout with poor visual hierarchy and navigation issues.

After: Fixed navigation, clear error handling, and optimized layout for notch screens.

Final Reflection

Social/Ethical Implications: We ensured our system promotes healthy habits rather than obsessive tracking by focusing on positive reinforcement (XP) instead of penalizing missed days heavily.

Use of AI: Generative AI was used strictly according to permissions to assist in debugging CSS flexbox issues and generating SQL mock data (Vibe Coding logs are included in the GitHub /ai-logs folder).
